Optimized Merchant's Operations Through Homepage Redesign

Company

GoTo (Gojek/GoFood Merchants)

Year

2025

Role

Product Designer

Team

PM, Engineers, UX Researcher, UX Writer

Overview

The homepage is the first and main entry point of the GoFood merchant app. Over time, without clear governance, teams had freely added data points, nudges, and banners to it, making it cluttered and unscalable. What should have been a useful starting point had become just another screen to tap through.

Problem

Key merchant actions were buried deep inside nested entry points. Toggling operational hours required scrolling down to the feature tiles, opening the outlet tile, then finding the toggle. Setting up menu and stock required going even deeper. On top of this, the homepage showed no meaningful performance indicators, leaving merchants with no way to quickly understand how their business was doing or what to act on.

Solution

We gathered merchant feedback on their homepage pain points and ran competitive benchmarking across direct and indirect competitors to understand how other platforms surface their home screens. This was important because GoFood merchants use other products too, and their expectations are shaped by those experiences.

The core design decision was shifting the homepage from a feature dumping ground into a central hub for restaurant operations. We surfaced the most-used actions upfront based on usage data, and added essential business metrics (ratings, order funnel, transactions, and balance) so merchants can get a clear read on their performance and take action without diving into sub-pages.

Outcome

Merchants responded positively, appreciating that key features were now easier and faster to reach. Feature adoption increased for the actions we brought upfront, confirming that merchants genuinely needed them accessible. We also saw a lift in ads and promo participation, a direct result of surfacing order funnel metrics alongside recommendations to run promotions and ads to improve performance.

Learning points

This project was fulfilling on two levels: it improved the merchant experience and gave us the opportunity to declutter and rethink how information is structured on the app's most visible surface. The work doesn't end at launch though. The next steps are establishing governance so the homepage stays scalable, and exploring how AI can surface rich, personalized insights based on each merchant's own performance data.
